About Me

I think MySpace missed an opportunity by not providing its old users access to their profile as the user left it. Side swiped hair, mirror pics in moms bathroom, t-shirts. They could of owned being the pioneers of social media and embracing the bad graphics of the early days. Instead of trying to capitalize on a music social media platform filled with ads that no one needs, they could have capitalized on 2005-2009 nostalgia, which is super strong now that Millenials are in our 30s. Tides would have turned and we would have visited what we abandoned, as long as it was still like it used to be. Revisted old messages, pictures, comments. Our profiles would be a snap shot in time from what our lives were like when we last logged in, in around 2009. MySpace changing the UI is somewhat expected, they lost Tom etc. etc. I do still believe that since the site was obsolete it would have been more beneficial for them to keep the old UI and really commit to the memory lane marketing strategy. Either way, MySpace went a step further and went as far as deleting your content after leaving it in something called "mixes" for a while. It's like they wanted to ditch the "abandoned social media" repuation, which doesn't work..instead of embracing that they hold ALL of our little emo phase content in their MySpace hands. I don't get it.

I decided to create the earliest MySpace UI from old pics I found, it is pretty close to the original! I love MySpace regardless of the ad ridden site they have become. They were the link I had to my friends I had just moved away from, and customizing my profile was what showed me HTML for the first time, and influenced me to do what I do now.
